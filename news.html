Research Featured by Khalifa University

My work on event-based panoptic segmentation was recently highlighted by Khalifa University in an article showcasing advances in robotic grasping and autonomous perception. The feature discusses our approach to combining neuromorphic event-based data with deep learning architectures to improve segmentation accuracy and robustness in cluttered environments.

The article covers our contributions, including the development of asynchronous event-based segmentation models and the broader impact on robotics and intelligent systems.
